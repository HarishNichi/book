INT. KANJIRAM ACADEMY – MAIN GATE – DAY (CONTINUOUS)
Anirudh stood near the front of the long, winding line of hopefuls. The grand gate of Kanjiram Academy loomed behind the stern-faced instructors, a promise of power and prestige. Around him, the other applicants, predominantly noble-born, fidgeted in their fine tunics and polished boots, their faces a mixture of nervousness and entitlement. Anirudh, in his simple, patched clothes, was a stark contrast, drawing uncomfortable, scrutinizing glances.
INSTRUCTOR
(Voice like grinding stone) Name?
ANIRUDH
Anirudh. No last name.
A collective intake of breath rippled through the line. Faces that had merely been dismissive now twisted into expressions of outright scorn and pity.
Whispers, sharp and sudden, erupted around Anirudh, though no one dared to speak directly to him.
"Orphan."
"Untitled."
"Worthless."
"He thinks he can stand among us?"
"A nobody."
Anirudh felt the sting of their words but held his gaze steady on the instructor, his jaw set.
INSTRUCTOR
(Looking him up and down, a flicker of something unreadable in their eyes) We’ll see if you survive the Trials. Step forward when called.
The instructor gestured for the next applicant, dismissing Anirudh with a final, cold look. Anirudh moved to the side, the weight of the whispered judgements pressing in, but his resolve remained unbroken. The Trials. His chance to prove them wrong.
