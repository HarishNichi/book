The enchanted forest was a maze of shifting trees and illusory paths. Rohan, in his arrogance, charged ahead, ignoring Anirudh's warnings. He soon found himself ensnared in a patch of magical vines that tightened with his every struggle.

Anirudh, who had been observing the patterns of the forest, found the source of the magic - a pulsating, glowing mushroom. He carefully destroyed it with a well-aimed rock, and the vines loosened their grip on Rohan.

Rohan, humbled and begrudgingly grateful, was forced to admit that Anirudh's resourcefulness was more valuable than he had thought. For the first time, he looked at Anirudh not as an orphan, but as a capable, if unconventional, ally.
