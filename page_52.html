The Orphan's Shadow

Page 52

The air crackled with an energy that was both exhilarating and terrifying. It wasn't just the physical strain of the ascent; it was the palpable sense of being watched, of pushing against an unseen force that sought to deter them. Elara's hand tightened on Kael's as they navigated a particularly treacherous ledge, the wind whipping at their cloaks like angry spirits.

Below, the valley was a patchwork of deep shadows and slivers of moonlight, a world they were leaving behind. Above, the peak loomed, a silent, imposing sentinel against the star-dusted sky. The ancient symbols carved into the rock face seemed to glow faintly, pulsing with a slow, rhythmic light that mirrored the beat of Elara's own heart.

Kael paused, his eyes scanning the rocky terrain ahead. "The path narrows here. We need to be careful. One false step..."

"I know," Elara finished, her voice barely a whisper against the wind. The gravity of their situation pressed down on them, heavier than the backpacks they carried. They were on a knife's edge, between the known world and whatever lay hidden at the summit.

A loose stone skittered down the mountainside with a sudden clatter, echoing in the stillness. Both of them froze, their senses heightened, listening for any other sign of movement. The mountain remained silent, but the brief sound had amplified their unease.

They continued their climb, each movement deliberate, each breath measured. The cold seeped into their bones, but the adrenaline coursing through their veins kept them going. They were close now. The air grew thinner, and the silence deepened, broken only by the sound of their own footsteps and the relentless wind.
