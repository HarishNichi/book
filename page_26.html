The Orphan's Shadow - Page 26

Silence stretched in the dormitory, broken only by the sounds of the other new initiates shifting in their own bunks. Anirudh lay still, every muscle aching, the dull throb of bruises a constant reminder of the trials. The air was heavy with anticipation, the fate of every participant hanging in the balance.

Then, a voice echoed through the dormitory, amplified and clear, seemingly coming from nowhere and everywhere at once. It was the voice of one of the lead instructors, devoid of emotion, delivering the final judgment.

INSTRUCTOR (V.O.)

The Trial of Initiation is concluded.

Out of the fifty candidates who began, only those who demonstrated the required skill, endurance, and spirit have been deemed worthy.

Only ten will be accepted into the esteemed Kanjiram Academy this year.

Anirudh held his breath, the words sinking in. Ten. Out of fifty. The odds had been stacked against him from the start, a nameless orphan competing against the well-trained children of nobility. His grip tightened on the pendant beneath his worn tunic.

The voice continued, reading out the names of the successful candidates. Each name was met with muted relief or quiet satisfaction from different corners of the dormitory. Anirudh listened intently, his heart pounding against his ribs.
