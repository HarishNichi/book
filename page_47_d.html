Days turned into a week. The three prisoners, Elara, Kaelen, and Elias, shared what little food they were given and whispered through the bars of their cells, piecing together a plan. Elias, with his keen observational skills, had noticed a pattern in the guards' routines. Kaelen, with his strength, would be the muscle. And Elara, with her connection to the subtle flows of magic, would be the key.

"There is a weak point in the wall of my cell," Kaelen whispered one night, his voice a low rumble. "I can feel a draft. If I can break through, I can get to the guard's lever."

Elara focused her senses, feeling the faint hum of magic in the air. "The locks on these cells are not just mechanical," she said. "They are sealed with a magical ward. I can try to disrupt it, but it will be risky. It will alert the guards."

They knew they had only one chance. They would have to act quickly and decisively. Their lives, and the fate of the mysterious Child of the Crimson Pendant, depended on it.
