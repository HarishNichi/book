The Orphan's Shadow - Page 33

Anirudh scanned the rows of ancient tomes, the air thick with the scent of aged paper and forgotten dust. His fingers traced the spines, searching for anything that felt... different. He was looking for answers the instructors wouldn't provide, for the truth behind the symbol on his pendant, the only link to a past shrouded in mystery.

The Orphan's Shadow - Page 14

The sun climbed higher, casting long shadows across the Stone Arena. With each passing challenge, the circle of hopefuls grew smaller. Dust and exhaustion clung to the remaining students. What had started as fifty vibrant, eager faces was now a grim collection of perhaps twenty, their earlier confidence replaced by weary determination.

Every stumble, every fall, every student carried away by medics, tightened the knot of tension in the air. The instructors watched with impassive faces, their gaze sharp, evaluating not just skill, but willpower. The final challenges loomed, a stark reminder that only the truly exceptional, the truly relentless, would earn a place within Kanjiram Academy.

Anirudh, though aching in every muscle and nursing fresh bruises, felt a surge of grim satisfaction. He was still standing. Many who had scoffed at him at the gate were already gone, their fine clothes and easy smiles forgotten in the crucible of the trials. The noise of the crowd, once a distant murmur, now felt closer, the anticipation for the final stage palpable.
