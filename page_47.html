Elara pushed further into the darkness, the faint light of her lantern casting eerie, dancing shadows on the uneven walls. The tunnel narrowed, forcing her to move single file, her hand trailing along the cool, rough stone.

A low, guttural sound echoed from ahead. Elara froze, her heart pounding against her ribs. It wasn't the sound of dripping water or shifting rock. It was a sound of deliberate movement, of something large and powerful navigating the confined space. She extinguished her lantern, plunging the tunnel into complete blackness, relying on her other senses to guide her.

The air grew colder, carrying with it a faint, unsettling hum. It felt like the air before a storm, thick with unseen energy. The guttural sounds grew louder, closer. Elara pressed herself against the damp wall, trying to make herself as small as possible, her hand instinctively going to the hilt of the dagger she kept strapped to her leg.

A flicker of movement at the edge of her limited vision. A shadow, deeper than the surrounding darkness, detached itself from the wall. It moved with a fluid, unnatural grace, silent despite its apparent size. Elara held her breath, every muscle tense, ready to spring away or strike if necessary.

The shadow paused, and for a terrifying moment, Elara felt as though it was looking directly at her, sensing her presence in the suffocating darkness. A low growl, a rumble that vibrated through the stone beneath her feet, confirmed her fears. Whatever this was, it was alive, and it knew she was there.

She had to move, to find a way past this creature before it decided to investigate further. The tunnel offered little room to maneuver.
