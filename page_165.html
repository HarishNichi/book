Anirudh was wary at first. He had learned to be cautious, to trust no one. But there was a sincerity in Elara's eyes, a strength in Kaelen's demeanor, and a wisdom in Elias's words that he could not deny. He listened to their story, to the tale of his parents, of the prophecy, of the destiny that awaited him.

He finally understood. He was not just an orphan, a warrior, a legend. He was a symbol of hope, a beacon of light in a world threatened by darkness. He was the Child of the Crimson Pendant, and he had a war to win.
