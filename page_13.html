The Orphan's Shadow - Page 13

From the elevated platform overlooking the arena, the instructors watched with impassive faces, their eyes scanning the struggling hopefuls below. Among them stood the stern-faced INSTRUCTOR who had registered Anirudh.

"He's lasted longer than I expected," another instructor, a man with a neatly trimmed beard, remarked, his tone tinged with surprise as he gestured towards Anirudh navigating a difficult obstacle.

The stern Instructor offered a curt nod. "Determination can take one far, Master Elara. But grit alone does not make an alchemist... or a warrior worthy of Kanjiram."

Nearby, a group of privileged onlookers, parents of some of the noble-born students, scoffed. "An orphan? Seriously? They're letting just anyone participate now?" A woman fanned herself languidly, her expression one of utter disdain.

Their dismissive whispers were a low hum against the sounds of the trial, a stark contrast to Anirudh's silent, grueling effort in the arena. For some, his very presence was an unwelcome anomaly.

Back on the platform, Master Elara watched Anirudh for a moment longer, a flicker of something unreadable in his eyes. "Perhaps," he murmured, more to himself than his colleague, "sometimes an anomaly is precisely what is needed."

But the stern Instructor had already turned his attention to another participant, dismissing Anirudh from his thoughts as quickly as he had registered him.

The trial continued, a brutal winnowing process under the indifferent eyes of those who held the keys to the academy's gates.
